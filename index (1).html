Surfacing hidden skills. Creating bridge connections. Decentralizing trust.

1. The Problem

2. The Unique Insight

Two research-backed insights reveal why talented people stay stuck, and point to a powerful solution.

Insight 1: Below the Iceberg

When you see an iceberg, only about 10% is visible above water. The other 90% is hidden below, invisible but real.

Skills work the same way. Employers see degrees and certificates (10%). But skills that predict workplace success are learned through life: Dependability. Problem-solving. Time management. Communication. Resilience.

We call these "building block skills." A delivery worker with a 4.8 rating has proven time management. An MNREGA worker with perfect attendance has proven dependability. Real skills, invisible credentials.

Insight 2: The Power of Bridge Connections

Counterintuitive finding from 50 years of research: acquaintances open more doors than close friends when it comes to jobs.

Close friends know what you know, an echo chamber. But acquaintances, what researchers call "consequential strangers", move in different circles. They bring unexpected opportunities from worlds you've never accessed.

Privileged youth have built-in bridge connections: alumni networks, parents' contacts. Those at the bottom have strong family ties but lack bridges to orthogonal worlds.

Research: Strength of Weak Ties (Granovetter, 1973)

Most people find jobs through acquaintances, not close friends. Confirmed across 55 countries. Strong ties provide emotional support. Bridge connections open unexpected doors.

The Solution: One Action, Two Results

What if one action could simultaneously:

1. Surface a hidden skill (bring it above the iceberg)

2. Create a new bridge connection (link to an orthogonal world)

That action is a badge, given by someone who has witnessed your skill in action.

Every badge = Skill made visible + New bridge connection to a different world

What Is a Badge?

A digital credential that says: "I have personally witnessed this person demonstrate this skill."

Unlike institutional certificates, badges come from people who've observed the skill: supervisors, customers, mentors. Each badge is stored on a decentralized ledger: verifiable, tamper-proof, owned by the earner.

Every badge = credential (skill visible) + bridge connection (link to orthogonal world)

The Mentor Model: Harish & Akshay

How mentoring actually works

Harish (45, PSU Finance Manager) mentors Akshay (22, his maid's son who dropped out after Class 12).

The project: Every day, Harish sends Akshay stock prices of 5 companies via WhatsApp. Akshay records them in Excel by 10 AM.

What's assessed: Not finance knowledge. Accountability. Does he do it daily without reminders? Does he catch errors?

Building the playlist: After 3 weeks, add complexity: percentage changes, weekly averages. Each milestone = new badge.

The introduction: Harish tells a friend at an accounting firm: "I have a young man who's tracked stocks daily for 2 months. Never missed a day."

Result: Akshay gets a temp job. Harish's badge was his credential. Harish's introduction was his bridge connection to an orthogonal world.

Worker demonstrates skill > Mentor observes > Badge issued > Mentor makes introduction > Unexpected door opens

5. Why Now? Blockchain Enables Decentralized Trust

The concept of skill validation isn't new. What's new is the technology to decentralize it, making this viable for the first time.

Old Model: Centralized Trust
  • Only institutions issue credentials
  • Expensive and time-consuming
  • Can't verify informal learning
  • Credentials can be faked
  • Institution owns the record
  • No relationship created
New Model: Decentralized Trust
  • Anyone who witnesses can badge
  • Free, earned through actions
  • Life experience counts
  • Tamper-proof blockchain
  • Individual owns credentials
  • Every badge = new connection

Why Blockchain?

Decentralized Reputation Systems use distributed ledger technology so that no single entity controls reputation information:

  • Democratic Issuance: Anyone who witnesses a skill can issue a badge
  • Instant Verification: Employers verify badges are real
  • Tamper-Proof: Badges can't be faked or unfairly revoked
  • User-Owned: Workers own their credentials, portable forever

How Trust Builds Over Time

  1. Mentor reputation: Mentors who badge people who succeed build credibility
  2. Badge patterns: Multiple independent badges are more credible than one
  3. Outcome tracking: Success reflects back on badge-givers
  4. Community curation: The network validates trust, no single entity controls it
"In a networked world, trust is the most important currency." - Eric Schmidt

Real Stories

Vikram: Delivery to Logistics

Sunita: MNREGA to Government

Age 32, Rural Rajasthan, "Financially Strapped" > Stable role

Sunita has 100% MNREGA attendance for 3 years. An NGO helps her join. Supervisor badges her for Dependability. A retired teacher mentors literacy. The NGO coordinator introduces her to the Block Development Officer.

Result: Government data entry role. Regular hours, stability.

Rima: Domestic Worker to School Admissions

Age 26, Class 10 dropout, Rs 8,000 > Rs 18,000

Rima is a nanny, dependable, quick learner. Employers badge her for Reliability. Selects "Childcare" playlist. A daycare mentor completes her training, then bridges her to a play school.

Result: Admissions job at Rs 18,000/month with growth potential.
