The Trust Network

What if we could surface the hidden skills that millions already have, and connect them to opportunities they've never had access to?

The Concept

India will have greater than 98 crore working-age people by 2035. Millions have real skills learned through life, not school. But these skills are invisible to employers. They also lack bridge connections that span different worlds and open unexpected doors.

The Trust Network uses badges and playlists to make hidden skills visible, while creating the connections that lead to opportunities. Blockchain makes it trustworthy and democratic.

The Unique Insight

Two research-backed insights reveal why talented people stay stuck, and point to a powerful solution.

Below the Iceberg

When you see an iceberg, only about 10% is visible above water. The other 90% is hidden below, invisible but real.

Skills work the same way. Employers see degrees and certificates (10%). But skills that predict workplace success are learned through life: Dependability. Problem-solving. Time management. Resilience.

We call these "building block skills." A delivery worker with a 4.8 rating has proven time management. An MNREGA worker with perfect attendance has proven dependability. Real skills, invisible credentials.

The Power of Bridge Connections

Counterintuitive finding from 50 years of research: acquaintances open more doors than close friends when it comes to jobs.

Close friends know what you know, an echo chamber. But acquaintances, what researchers call "consequential strangers," move in different circles. They bring unexpected opportunities from worlds you've never accessed.

Privileged youth have built-in bridge connections: alumni networks, parents' contacts. Those at the bottom have strong family ties but lack bridges to orthogonal worlds.

The Solution: One Action, Two Results

What Is a Badge?

A digital credential that says: "I have personally witnessed this person demonstrate this skill."

Unlike institutional certificates, badges come from people who've observed the skill: supervisors, customers, mentors.

Each badge is stored on a decentralized ledger: verifiable, tamper-proof, owned by the earner.

Badges Create Connections

Every badge comes from a person. That person now knows you and believes in you.

The mentor who badges you might know someone in logistics. The customer who vouches for you might work at a company hiring.

Each badge = credential + bridge to an orthogonal world.

The Mentor Model: Harish & Akshay

How mentoring actually works

Harish (45, PSU Finance Manager) mentors Akshay (22, his maid's son who dropped out after Class 12).

The project: Every day, Harish sends Akshay stock prices of 5 companies via WhatsApp. Akshay records them in Excel by 10 AM.

What's assessed: Not finance knowledge. Accountability. Does he do it daily without reminders? Does he catch errors?

Building the playlist: After 3 weeks, add complexity: percentage changes, weekly averages. Each milestone = new badge.

The introduction: Harish tells a friend at an accounting firm: "I have a young man who's tracked stocks daily for 2 months. Never missed a day."

Result: Akshay gets a temp job. Harish's badge was his credential. Harish's introduction was his bridge connection.

The Core Interaction

Worker demonstrates skill → Mentor observes → Badge issued → Mentor makes introduction → Unexpected door opens

This creates a flywheel: successful placements build mentor reputation → more workers seek that mentor → employers trust that mentor's recommendations.

Why Now?

The concept of skill validation isn't new. What's new is the technology to decentralize it, making this viable for the first time.

Old Model: Centralized Trust
  • Only institutions issue credentials
  • Expensive and time-consuming
  • Can't verify informal learning
  • Credentials can be faked
  • Institution owns the record
  • No relationship created
New Model: Decentralized Trust
  • Anyone who witnesses can badge
  • Free, earned through actions
  • Life experience counts
  • Tamper-proof blockchain
  • Individual owns credentials
  • Every badge = new connection

Why Blockchain?

Decentralized Reputation Systems use distributed ledger technology so that no single entity controls reputation information:

Democratic Issuance

Anyone who witnesses a skill can badge

Instant Verification

Employers verify badges are real

Tamper-Proof

Badges can't be faked or revoked unfairly

User-Owned

Workers own their credentials forever

How Trust Builds Over Time

Mentor reputation: Mentors who badge people who succeed build credibility. Their badges carry more weight.

Badge patterns: Multiple independent badges are more credible than one.

Outcome tracking: Success reflects back on badge-givers.

Community curation: The network validates trust, no single entity controls it.

Technology now allows us to decentralize credentials, just as it decentralized currency and contracts.
